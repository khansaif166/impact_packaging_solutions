How We Work

Our Capabilities

From design planning to dispatch readiness, our packaging workflow is structured around performance and consistency.

Process

From Brief to Batch Release

Each stage of our process is designed to align material choices, production steps, and quality expectations.

1

Design & Pre-Press

Artwork preparation, packaging format planning, and print-readiness review.

2

Material Selection & Sourcing

Substrate planning based on barrier, appearance, handling, and application needs.

3

Printing

Artwork and print execution are reviewed for clean presentation, registration, and brand consistency.

4

Lamination

Layer bonding to support structure performance, visual finish, and product protection.

5

Slitting & Pouching

Final conversion into roll formats or pouch structures as per order requirements.

6

Quality Testing & Dispatch

Inspection checkpoints and dispatch preparation before release.

Print Capability

High-Impact Print Quality

Our print-focused workflow supports clear artwork handling, consistent visual output, and packaging presentation suited to each product format.

Materials

Material Capability Range

Substrate combinations can be aligned to performance, finish, handling, and product requirements.

BOPP

Commonly used for print surfaces and flexible laminate structures.

PET

Supports structural stability, print quality, and thermal performance.

PE

Often used for sealing layers and flexible handling properties.

CPP

Film option for seal performance and application-specific laminate design.

LDPE

Commonly used as a sealant layer in flexible laminate structures, supporting flexibility, heat sealing, and smooth pouch conversion.

Foil

Applied in structures that require stronger barrier-focused packaging.

LLDPE

Offers improved toughness, puncture resistance, and seal strength, making it useful for pouches that need better handling durability.

Quality Assurance

Every Roll, Every Pouch, Every Batch Tested.

  • Seal strength checks based on format and application requirement
  • Barrier-related verification checkpoints where relevant
  • Print registration and visual consistency review
  • Batch-level checks before dispatch release
